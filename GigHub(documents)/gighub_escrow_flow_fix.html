Problem statement trap & real-life validation

The "problem" is deeper than late payments. Here are the structural traps that a surface-level solution misses — validated by Malaysia-specific research.

Trap 1 — "Just build an escrow app"
Escrow alone doesn't solve the verification problem. If a client puts money in escrow and then refuses to release it claiming bad work, you've just moved the dispute — not resolved it. Without GLM comparing the deliverable to the original brief, you have no arbiter. This is why Fiverr and Upwork still have massive fraud and dispute rates despite having escrow.
Trap 2 — "Gig workers need minimum wage"
The Ministry of Human Resources confirmed the RM1,700 minimum wage doesn't directly apply to gig workers — a separate Gig Consultative Council (MPGIG, first meeting April 3 2026) handles income rates. The real problem isn't wage level; it's that freelancers can't prove their income to banks. No payslip, no EPF record, no loan. A platform that generates formal income history solves a more urgent problem.
Trap 3 — "One rate fits all freelancers"
An e-hailing driver, a childcare provider, and a freelance translator all fall under the same Gig Workers Act 2025, but their cost structures are entirely different. GLM can dynamically analyse market rates by sector, location, and complexity — producing personalised income benchmarks, not a single blunt number.
Trap 4 — "The law has fixed the problem"
Malaysia's Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) flagged that the Gig Workers Bill is still missing provisions for wage guarantees. APAD's May 2025 shutdown of InDrive and Maxim showed regulatory crackdowns can instantly wipe out a freelancer's income. The law created a framework but not a safety net.
Trap 5 — "Confidential work is unverifiable"
When jobs are confidential, platforms instinctively give up on verification. GigHub solves this with end-to-end encryption + wrapper verification: file metadata, SHA-256 hash proofs, and timestamps confirm delivery without exposing content. GLM assesses brief fulfilment using structure only — never reads the underlying IP.

Why GLM is non-removable

Removability test: if GLM is off, the platform cannot (1) validate whether a brief is specific enough to be enforceable, (2) score whether a deliverable fulfils a brief, (3) generate a neutral dispute summary, or (4) produce personalised income benchmarks. The escrow flow breaks at steps 2 and 3. The platform becomes a file-sharing tool with a payment hold.

GLM functions in the core loop

Brief validation
Scores brief completeness (0–100), flags vague requirements, asks clarifying questions before any freelancer sees the job. Threshold: ≥70 required before a job goes live. Without GLM, any brief goes live regardless of quality.
Milestone scoring
Compares deliverable metadata and activity log against the agreed brief. Produces a structured pass/partial/fail verdict. This is the escrow release trigger — without it, there's no automated basis for fund release.
Dispute arbitration
Reads brief vs submission match score, activity trail, rejection reason, and client dispute history. Generates a neutral ruling summary. Flags bad-faith rejections. Human moderator reviews before final ruling within 48hr.
Income intelligence
Analyses completed project history, compares against market rate benchmarks by skill and sector, and generates a formal income statement PDF for bank loan applications.
Job matching
Reads freelancer portfolio and skills against open briefs, ranks fit using semantic understanding — not just keyword matching.

Honest assessment of GLM vs alternatives

Suitable GLM-4 handles all 5 functions above. Brief scoring, dispute summarisation, and job matching are text reasoning tasks where GLM-4 performs competitively at low cost.
Limitation GLM's vision model (glm-4v) is weaker for design quality review. For visual deliverables, rely on metadata verification + client sign-off rather than GLM visual assessment.
Hackathon fit Brief match scorer + dispute summariser are the two highest-impact features to demo — both are well within GLM-4's capabilities and prove non-removability.

Feature set

1. Structured brief builder + GLM validation
Company fills a structured brief form (deliverable type, format, word count/dimensions, deadline, reference examples). GLM scores specificity and asks clarifying questions for gaps. A brief must score ≥70 before it is visible to freelancers. Prevents unenforceable contracts from the start.
2. Freelancer acceptance + escrow trigger
Once GLM approves a brief, it is listed on the marketplace. When a freelancer accepts the job, the platform prompts the company to fund escrow before work begins. This sequence — accept first, then escrow — ensures no freelancer starts work without guaranteed payment, while no company is locked into escrow before a match exists.
3. Milestone system
Project amount broken into up to 5 milestones. 50/50 split option available to ease SME friction. 72-hour auto-release if client doesn't respond to a submission — prevents ghosting. Watermarked previews for design work prevent IP theft before payment.
4. GLM milestone verification
On submission, GLM receives the original brief, file metadata (size, format, word count, dimensions), submission timestamp log, and activity trail. Produces a 0–100 brief match score with breakdown by requirement. Triggers auto-release if score ≥ threshold and client is unresponsive.
5. GLM dispute arbitration
If client rejects: GLM reads brief vs submission match score, activity trail evidence, and rejection reason. Generates a neutral ruling summary. Flags bad-faith patterns (vague rejection, rejection after deadline, history of disputes). Human moderator reviews GLM summary and issues final ruling within 48hr.
6. Income intelligence dashboard
Freelancer-facing: monthly/annual earnings summary, market rate benchmarks by skill (from GLM analysis of platform data), formal income statement PDF for bank loan applications. Directly solves the loan access problem under the Gig Workers Act 2025 framework.
E2E encrypted
7. Confidential work — end-to-end encrypted delivery

All deliverables on GigHub are treated as confidential by default. Files are encrypted on the freelancer's device before upload (AES-256-GCM), pass through GigHub's servers as an opaque encrypted blob, and are automatically hard-deleted from Cloudflare R2 after 72 hours. GigHub never holds decryptable content.

1
Client-side encryption
Freelancer's browser encrypts the file using AES-256-GCM with a symmetric key derived from the job keypair before any bytes leave the device.
2
Encrypted upload to R2
The encrypted blob is uploaded to Cloudflare R2. GigHub's server receives and stores only the ciphertext — it cannot read the file content at any point.
3
SHA-256 hash recorded
A SHA-256 hash of the encrypted file is stored immutably in PostgreSQL as proof-of-delivery without revealing its contents.
4
Client-side decryption
The client downloads the encrypted blob and decrypts it locally using the shared job key. GigHub never participates in decryption.
5
72-hour auto-delete
R2 lifecycle rule hard-deletes the encrypted blob 72 hours after upload. The SHA-256 hash in PostgreSQL remains as the permanent audit record.
6
GLM uses metadata only
For milestone scoring and dispute analysis, GLM receives only the brief text, file metadata, and activity log — never file content.
Compliant with Malaysia's PDPA and Gig Workers Act 2025. GigHub cannot be compelled to produce file content it has never decrypted.

🗄️
PostgreSQL (primary)
Users, jobs, milestones, escrow state machine, submission logs, audit trail, GLM scores, dispute records, SHA-256 hash proofs. Milestone state transitions are atomic and auditable.
⚡
Redis (session + queue)
Session tokens, real-time notification queue, escrow lock (prevent double-release race condition), GLM response cache (brief scores cached 24hr)
☁️
Cloudflare R2 (file storage)
Stores encrypted blobs only — GigHub never holds plaintext content. Zero egress fees. R2 lifecycle rule hard-deletes all files 72 hours after upload. SHA-256 hash retained in PostgreSQL as permanent proof-of-delivery.

Data safety

Regulatory baseline — PDPA + Gig Workers Act 2025
Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) governs how freelancer and client data is collected, stored, and processed. The Gig Workers Act 2025 mandates payment transparency and service agreement documentation. Because GigHub never decrypts file content, it cannot be compelled to produce content it has never held in plaintext.

End-to-end encryption model

Core architecture
Files are encrypted in the browser using the Web Crypto API (AES-256-GCM) before upload. GigHub's servers receive only ciphertext. The symmetric key is derived from a per-job keypair exchanged between freelancer and client — GigHub never holds the key.
SHA-256 hash of the encrypted blob is stored immutably in PostgreSQL as the proof-of-delivery record for dispute arbitration, escrow release, and legal compliance.
72-hour hard-delete via Cloudflare R2 lifecycle rule removes the encrypted blob automatically. The hash record in PostgreSQL is permanent and append-only.

GLM context safety

GLM prompts assembled server-side only — API key never exposed to browser. PII stripped from all prompts (names and emails replaced with role tokens: freelancer_1, client_1).
GLM receives only: brief text, file metadata extracted pre-encryption, activity logs, and submission timestamps. File content is never sent to GLM under any circumstances.
GLM responses stored in PostgreSQL for milestone and dispute decisions only — not used for training or third-party sharing.

Payment data

Card and banking data never stored on the platform — delegated to Stripe or iPay88 (PCI-DSS compliant). Platform stores only: transaction ID, amount, status, and timestamp. Escrow is funded after freelancer acceptance and held as a ledger entry in PostgreSQL until milestone release.

Access control

JWT with 15-minute access tokens + 7-day refresh tokens in HttpOnly cookies. Role-based access: freelancer sees only their own jobs; company sees only jobs they posted; admin sees all for dispute resolution. PostgreSQL row-level security via user_id partitioning. All API routes validated with Zod schemas before database writes.
